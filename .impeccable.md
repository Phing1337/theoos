## Design Context

### Users
TheoOS is for Nick and Kayla coordinating Theo's day-to-day co-parenting schedule. The primary context is quick phone use: checking who has Theo, making a coverage or swap request, adding a note, and exporting a calendar item when useful. Tablet support matters for a more relaxed planning view, while desktop only needs to remain competent and readable.

### Brand Personality
Calm, tactile, practical. The interface should feel like a warm paper schedule with enough polish to trust it, but not like a corporate productivity dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Mobile-first, light, sage-toned, and calendar-centered. Use color only semantically for custody ownership, request states, and meaningful system feedback. Prefer quiet hierarchy, reduced typography variety, realistic soft shadows, and subtle touch motion over decorative flourishes.

### Design Principles
- Phone is the primary surface; every feature must be comfortable at 320-430px.
- Tablet earns a real two-column planning layout instead of just scaling phone UI.
- Desktop is constrained and useful, not a stretched wide app.
- Calendar ownership color should be obvious at a glance.
- Motion should acknowledge touch and state changes without feeling playful or distracting.
